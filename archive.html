29th June 2006 - Now the Stage 1 mods have been completed on the 200SX it was time to see what improvements had been made by getting it back on the dyno. PowerStation was the obvious choice so first thing this morning we get the car back on there... since sorting the timing from the last dyno run we know that the massive dip at 4k has gone, and since setting up the boost to 1.1bar the car has felt very much better than before, so we were expecting a decent power increase, and we weren't disappointed! The pictures, movie and dyno graph can be see here... but the results were 258bhp and 242lb/ft, an increase of 44bhp and 62 lb/ft... if only 205 tuning were this easy!!



25th June 2006 - With the 205 away we decided to get on and fit the full 'Stage 1' kit to the S13 along with a FMIC. Fortunately the car was already fitted with a Stage 1 chip and a turbo-back exhaust so all we had to do were fit cooler plugs, air filter, fuel pump and a boost controller set to ~15psi. We had also got hold of a SAAB Intercooler which can be fitted in place just in front of the radiator, with the correct pipe work it fits quite nicely! We also took the change to remove the Air-Con seeing as it's never worked and weighs a fair bit. Loads of pictures of the mods are available here... we just need to set the boost up to 15psi and get the car back on the rollers!

12th June 2006 - Today the project 200SX had a date with a rolling road, obviously the PowerStation dyno as we work above it so it's nice and easy! We weren't expecting great things as the cars done 190k miles and had been sat for 18 months. There were already a few mods on the car, the turbo back exhaust and a Stage 1 chip, also we'd drilled the standard airbox out but other than that it seemed pretty standard. I thought the boost was running a little more than standard and this was proved on the rollers... a nice 214bhp and 181lb/ft were produced at 0.8 bar! So all seems good... until you look at the graph and see the MASSIVE dip at around 4k rpm and there is some cause for concern. A PDF of the graph is available here. We have a few things to sort out so hopefully that will solve the dip... aiming to get back on the dyno in the next couple of weeks once we've done the other mods to the car.

7th June 2006 - 7th June 2006 - Had a good look over the 200SX last night and came across a major bit of luck... turns out the ECU has already got a Horsam Developments Stage 1 chip fitted! We were going to be getting one of those soon anyhow but this has saved us around £100 which is superb! We also did the free airbox modification of removing the 'S' pipe and drilling the bottom of the airbox. Just got to remove the resonator box and hopefully there might be some induction noise. There is already a TIM (ick!) boost gauge fitted to the car and on that it seems to be producing 0.9 bar but I don't really trust such a cheap and nasty gauge so we'll check the boost properly on the dyno.
