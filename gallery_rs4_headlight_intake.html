Audi B5 RS4 | Headlight Intake | 15/08/12

On my old 205 Mi16 track car I'd made a neat air filter out of an old headlight which was feeding a set of Jenveys. I'd never really given it much thought for the RS4 until I started to get into the idea of more outings on track, so I decided to make a direct cold air intake from an old headlight to feed the K&N Filter. So I picked up a used headlight, an air intake from Merlin Motorsport and some 83mm ducting from SiliconHoses.com then set about cutting things up!

Here I forgot to get the camera out and take any pictures of cutting through the reflective inner section and securing the inlet/ducting to the headlight. Basically I used a Dremel to cut the reflector which I first thought was metal but is in fact a form of fiberglass which cut easily but made a LOT of dust! Then it was just a case of securing the reflector due to the loss of the mounting points (I used one screw through the bottom and a cable tie on the side) and threading the ducting though to the inlet trumpet. The trumpet itself came with a sticky backed mount so that was used to attach that to the headlight - job done.

No idea if it makes a difference but it can't hurt in the slightest! The indicator and dipped beam still work actually (though the dipped beam is not a HID in this headlight) so totally fine for a trackday but I'd not bother using it on the road as don't want to make the car stand out too much to the cops really.
