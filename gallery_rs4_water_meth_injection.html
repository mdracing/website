Audi B5 RS4 | Devils Own Water/Meth Injection | 30/05/12

I'd never considered Water/Meth injection before buying the B5 RS4 and joining the AudiSRS.com. There are many S4/RS4 owners who have fitted Water/Meth injection with some great results, and the outlay isn't vast so I figured I'd give it a go to help achieve my target on Phase 1 of mods.

The kit I went for is the DevilsOwn DVC-30 Progressive Kit with Dual Nozzle upgrade. This kit comes with their superb progressive controller which allows you to fine tune when the injection starts to happen and when you want it fully on, hence "progressive". Their more basic option is a basic "on/off" solenoid valve where you just pick one boost level for the system to activate.

Fitting Progressive Controller

The first step was to fit the progressive controller and get the wiring in place for the pump. This was done in the Summer of 2011 when I had a mate come over and help install the Podi Boost Gauge as they both needed access to similar components and wiring.

I've seen a few of these controllers installed just mounted to the dash but it's not something you need to touch and faff with once setup so I opted to mount it in a space above the fuses box in the end of the dash. I also took the decision to fit an on/off switch for the whole system so I can choose to run with or without Water/Meth as I know I won't use/need it that often.

The pump wiring is very simple indeed and is connected to the controller by the earth wire. Here we just ran it up to where the pump is going to be installed and left it there for the time being.

Fitting Nozzles

Nozzle location was my next task, and it only took 8 months to get around to sorting it! The advice from DevilsOwn is to fit the nozzles a minimum of 8" from the throttle body. Many B5 RS4 owners have just fitted the nozzles into the metal throttle body Y-Pipe as it's thick enough to drill and tap for the nozzles to screw straight in, however it's really a little too close to the throttle body so I didn't want to go that route.

So I decided to have bosses welded to the boost pipes (often referred to as the "pancake pipes" due to their flatness) which meant the rather fiddly task of removing them from the car. They are only held on by two 10mm bolts at the bottom and one allen head by the throttle body pipe but access is very tight indeed. I found removing the passenger side headlight gave better access to the pipe that side due to the radiator fan.

With the pipe removed I popped them over to the great chaps @ Forge Motorsport for a spot of fabrication. They did a superb job of welding some appropriate bosses to the pipes so it was just a case of re-fitting them, just as difficult if not more so than removing them!

To ensure there would be no boost leaks before the full install was finished off I just ran a pipe from one nozzle to the other.

Fitting Pump & Pipework

The last piece of the installation was to fit a new washer bottle, which is where I'm taking the mix from, run the pipework and wire up the pump. I had planned all along to use the washer bottle as the tank for the mix as it's very convenient and meth is found in normal windscreen washer fluid so it can still be used for that. The added benefit is the low water level sensor in the bottle will tell you when your running low on mix - perfect!

Guessing the bottle was as old as the car I figured a new one was best, so this was fitted along with the Wagner Sidemount Intercoolers as similar parts needed removing for access. It's a bit of a twist and fiddle affair to get the bottle out and new one in. We drilled a hole in the top of the tank which I fed the pump feed pipe down, this avoids the need for any seal on the bottle and no chance of leaks from the feed.

I wanted to keep the install as neat as possible, so decided to run the pipework from the pump to the nozzles under the Y-Pipe so it's pretty much hidden by the carbon engine covers. Since first fitting I have wrapped the pipe in heat-proof tape as the fitting guide does say to avoid running pipework near heat sources.

Finally the pump was positioned up next to the battery, all pipes were joined up and the pump wired to the battery live terminal. All and held in place with a zip tie which isn't pretty but does the job and it's all covered by the scuttle cover anyhow.

I'm really pleased with how the install has turned out, hasn't really changed the look under the bonnet which was my goal :)

Results

Wagner Sidemount Intercoolers so although I logged the inlet temps a few days before the install (75°C), the after results are with the wagners. However I did do two runs, one without the Water/Meth and one with, the results of inlet temps were as follows:

Water/Meth Off - 39°C
Water/Meth On - 29°C

This shows that with a 50/50 mix of Water/Meth the inlet temp dropped 10°C on a back-to-back run - result! Below is the dyno run with the Water/Meth on and the dyno graph with the inlet temps plotted is available here and note this is all still on the standard map.
